This Non-Disclosure Agreement (this "Agreement") is entered into as of the date of electronic acceptance below (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

Disclosing Party: Liminal Drip LTD, an Ohio limited liability company ("Company"); and

Receiving Party: The individual identified in the acceptance form below ("Recipient").

Company and Recipient may each be referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

WHEREAS, the Company possesses certain confidential and proprietary information relating to its products, services, technology, and business operations; and

WHEREAS, the Recipient desires to receive access to such information for the purpose of beta testing, evaluating, or otherwise engaging with the Company's products and services (the "Purpose");

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants herein and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by the Company to the Recipient, whether orally, in writing, electronically, visually, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

(a) software source code, object code, algorithms, data structures, architecture, designs, prototypes, and technical documentation;
(b) product plans, roadmaps, features (whether released or unreleased), user interface designs, and user experience workflows;
(c) business plans, financial information, pricing models, marketing strategies, customer lists, and partnership arrangements;
(d) trade secrets as defined under the Ohio Uniform Trade Secrets Act (§1333.61 et seq. of the Ohio Revised Code) and the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1836 et seq.);
(e) any information that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure.
2. Exclusions from Confidential Information

Confidential Information does not include information that the Recipient can demonstrate by competent evidence:

(a) was already in the public domain at the time of disclosure, or subsequently became publicly available through no fault or breach by the Recipient;
(b) was already known to the Recipient prior to disclosure, without obligation of confidentiality;
(c) was independently developed by the Recipient without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or
(d) was lawfully obtained from a third party who had no obligation of confidentiality to the Company.
3. Obligations of the Recipient

The Recipient agrees to:

(a) hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the Company;
(b) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described herein and for no other purpose;
(c) protect the Confidential Information using the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information, but in no event less than reasonable care;
(d) limit access to the Confidential Information to those of its employees, agents, or advisors who have a need to know for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those in this Agreement;
(e) promptly notify the Company in writing of any unauthorized use or disclosure of the Confidential Information of which the Recipient becomes aware.
4. Prohibition on Public Disclosure

The Recipient shall not, without the prior written consent of the Company, publicly disclose, discuss, or reference any aspect of the Confidential Information, the Company's products, or the Recipient's relationship with the Company, including but not limited to:

(a) posting screenshots, screen recordings, descriptions, or reviews on social media platforms, application review sites, forums, blogs, or any other publicly accessible medium;
(b) sharing or demonstrating the Company's products or materials to individuals who are not parties to this Agreement;
(c) discussing features, functionality, design, or performance of the Company's products in any public or semi-public setting.
5. Feedback and Intellectual Property Assignment

5.1 The Recipient may, during the course of the Purpose, provide suggestions, comments, ideas, improvements, feature requests, bug reports, or other feedback to the Company (collectively, "Feedback").

5.2 The Recipient hereby irrevocably assigns to the Company all right, title, and interest in and to any Feedback, including all intellectual property rights therein. The Recipient agrees that the Company is free to use, implement, disclose, reproduce, license, distribute, and otherwise commercialize any Feedback without restriction, attribution, or compensation to the Recipient.

5.3 The Recipient waives any moral rights or equivalent rights in the Feedback to the extent permitted by applicable law.

5.4 To the extent any assignment under this Section 5 is not effective under applicable law, the Recipient grants the Company a perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free, fully paid-up, exclusive license to use, modify, and exploit the Feedback in any manner.

6. No Rights Granted

Nothing in this Agreement grants the Recipient any license, ownership interest, or other right in or to the Confidential Information or any intellectual property of the Company. All Confidential Information remains the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

7. Compelled Disclosure

If the Recipient is compelled by law, regulation, or valid legal process to disclose any Confidential Information, the Recipient shall, to the extent legally permitted, provide the Company with prompt written notice of such requirement prior to disclosure, so that the Company may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy. The Recipient shall disclose only the minimum amount of Confidential Information required by such legal process.

8. Return and Destruction of Materials

Upon the Company's written request, or upon the termination or expiration of this Agreement, the Recipient shall promptly return or destroy all copies, notes, summaries, and other materials containing or derived from Confidential Information, and shall certify such return or destruction in writing.

9. Term and Survival

9.1 This Agreement shall remain in effect from the Effective Date until the earlier of: (a) written notice of termination by either Party with thirty (30) days' advance notice; or (b) two (2) years from the Effective Date.

9.2 The Recipient's obligations of confidentiality under this Agreement shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of two (2) years. Obligations with respect to trade secrets shall survive for as long as the information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

10. Remedies

The Recipient acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Company for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity, without the necessity of proving actual damages or posting a bond.
